        Shape

        3840 × 2160

        Your footage keeps its own aspect ratio — reframe vertical or cinemascope shots in Resolve after import.

        Cinemascope heads-up: 16:9 footage shows bars on the SIDES at first — for the classic cinema look, set Resolve’s Project Settings > Image Scaling > “Scale full frame with crop”. When you use “Build in DaVinci Resolve”, Monteur applies that crop setting for you — the manual step is only for downloaded files you import yourself.

        Length

        Whole song cuts across the entire track. Target length is usually better: Monteur picks the song’s strongest passage and ends on a musical boundary.

        Frame rate

        Not sure? 25 is a safe default in Europe, 30 in the US — it just needs to match your project in Resolve.

        Tell Claude the story

        Optional, one or two sentences. Claude reads it when composing the cut, and it doubles as the default context for Director’s notes in step 3.

        Claude reads the vision labels and your description, writes a story arc, casts every slot and titles the act breaks — the engine keeps every cut locked on the music. Runs over your Claude connection (with Claude Code: no extra cost). Sharpest when “Let Claude watch your clips” ran in step 1 — Monteur suggests it automatically after a labeled scan.

        Fine-tune — pace, transitions, clip order, repeats
        Cut pace

        Auto (recommended): Monteur cuts to the music, the clip’s content and the local tempo — lengths vary like a human edit. Set seconds only to force a feel.

        Transitions

        Auto (recommended): decided per cut — hard on the beat in action, a short dissolve on scene changes in calm passages, smash-to-black at act breaks.

        Deliberate silence (recommended): the song breaks under title cards and for one beat before the drop — silence is a weapon. Continuous: the song plays through everything.

        Clip order

        In order keeps clips as they were shot; best first puts your strongest moments on the loudest parts of the song.

        Cuts land a frame before the beat so the new shot hits ON the beat.

        Lets the same footage appear more than once when the cut runs longer than your good moments. Off, Monteur keeps every shot fresh.

        Ambience, risers and impacts planned as green markers on the timeline — each with a search query for your sound library.

        Monteur classifies your impacts/whooshes/risers offline and places them as real clips on their own audio track — riser into the drop, impact on the smash cuts. Filling this turns the SFX layer on.

                            Storage
                            
      
                            Monteur keeps small playback proxies of your clips so the player scrubs instantly. They’re a shared cache — deleting a project leaves them (another project may use the same clips), and the cache is capped and pruned automatically. Clear it any time to reclaim space; proxies rebuild on next playback.

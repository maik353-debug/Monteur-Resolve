        Create a first cut

        Step 1 of 3 — Your footage

        1. Your footage

        Tell Monteur where your video clips live. It watches every clip and finds the good parts — your files are never moved or changed.

        Tip: to copy a folder path — on macOS right-click the folder, hold Option, choose “Copy as Pathname”; on Windows hold Shift, right-click, choose “Copy as path”.

        Scanning your clips — long clips can take a while; progress appears in the monteur ui terminal.

        Usable Too dark Blurry Shaky

        2. Your music

        Pick the song your cut should follow. Monteur reads its beat and places every cut on the rhythm.

        Any common audio file works — .mp3, .wav or .m4a. Copy its path the same way as the folder.

        Not sure about frame rate? 25 is a safe default in Europe, 30 in the US — it just needs to match your project in Resolve.

        3. Your cut

        Monteur lines your best moments up with the beat and writes a small timeline file you can open in DaVinci Resolve.

        Cutting to the beat — Monteur reads your clips and the song, about a second per clip.

        Your cut is ready

          Next: import into DaVinci Resolve

          1. In Resolve, choose File → Import → Timeline… and pick the downloaded file.
          2. When Resolve asks where the media lives, point it at your footage folder.
          3. Press play — then fine-tune from there. FCPXML relinks most easily.

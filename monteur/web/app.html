        1

        Your footage

        Tell Monteur where your video clips live. It watches every clip and finds the good parts — your files are never moved or changed.

        Use Browse to pick the folder — or paste its path (macOS: right-click the folder, hold Option, “Copy as Pathname”; Windows: Shift + right-click, “Copy as path”).

        Needs the anthropic package and your Anthropic API key (ANTHROPIC_API_KEY) on the machine running monteur ui. Claude looks at one frame per good moment (about a cent per scan) and labels what it shows.

        Usable Too dark Blurry Shaky
        Search your footage — what did Claude see?

        Searches the labels Claude wrote during a “Let Claude watch” scan — offline and instant, no API call. Try “hero” for the money shots.

        2

        Music & options

        Pick the song your cut should follow and how the finished cut should feel. Monteur reads the song’s beat and places every cut on the rhythm.

        Soundtrack

        Any common audio file works — .mp3, .wav or .m4a.

        Not sure which song? Rank a folder

        Monteur listens to every song in the folder and scores how well it fits your scanned footage — beat clarity, length, tempo, drop and dynamics. When a Target length is set, songs are scored against it automatically.

        Sound in the finished cut

        Shape

        3840 × 2160

        Your footage keeps its own aspect ratio — reframe vertical or cinemascope shots in Resolve after import.

        Cinemascope heads-up: 16:9 footage shows bars on the SIDES at first — for the classic cinema look, set Resolve’s Project Settings > Image Scaling > “Scale full frame with crop”.

        Length

        Whole song cuts across the entire track. Target length is usually better: Monteur picks the song’s strongest passage and ends on a musical boundary.

        Style

        Cut pace

        How long each shot stays on screen in the fast parts — ~1 s feels snappy, ~4 s calm. The seconds are a target: Monteur rounds to whole beats, so cuts always stay on the music. Slow phases keep their proportion, and “Style default” lets each style set its own rhythm.

        Transitions

        Style default follows each style’s habits — calm phases dissolve, the Trailer smashes to black between acts (short black gaps with a “Title slot” marker for your text).

        Frame rate

        Not sure? 25 is a safe default in Europe, 30 in the US — it just needs to match your project in Resolve.

        Fine-tune — clip order, cut timing, repeats
        Clip order

        In order keeps clips as they were shot; best first puts your strongest moments on the loudest parts of the song.

        Cuts land a frame before the beat so the new shot hits ON the beat.

        Lets the same footage appear more than once when the cut runs longer than your good moments. Off, Monteur keeps every shot fresh.

        Ambience, risers and impacts planned as green markers on the timeline — each with a search query for your sound library.

              Publish kit — the upload package for this cut

              Uploading a video means writing a title, a description, chapters and tags, and hunting for a thumbnail — the kit drafts all of that from the cut above so publishing takes minutes, not an evening. It writes one publish.md with title suggestions and a short description (drafted by Claude when your Anthropic API key is set or Claude Code — the claude command — is installed; otherwise from an honest offline template that says so), YouTube chapter timestamps taken from the cut’s own scene changes, a ready-to-paste tag list, and real thumbnail stills of your strongest moments in a thumbs/ folder. A planned SFX layer adds its sound-design shopping list with Artlist search links.

              Where the kit lands: only publish.md and thumbs/ are written into this folder (it’s created if needed) — a publish folder next to your footage works well. “Write publish kit” drafts from the cut exactly as shown above, so revise first, publish after.
